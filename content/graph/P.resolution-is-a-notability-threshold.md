# Resolution Is a Notability Threshold

`Pattern` · `geography_cartography` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

The boring, blasé rules — map units, raster resolution, the cell size of the grid — are 'the entire framework of the irreality.' A raster cell is the smallest documentable unit of space: below the cell size, a thing does not exist — the meteor in the small Chilean town falls between the grid's teeth exactly the way it falls below Wikipedia's notability bar. Grid geometry and the notability threshold are the *same border-drawing operation* at two scales: each decides what is real enough to be a unit at all, and each launders that decision into something that looks like neutral measurement. The resolution is not a technical setting; it is the projection deciding what it is willing to lie about. This is why the sub-Wikipedia / sub-cell scale matters — 'the smallest unit of information is an idea, not an article' — and why fixing the resolution downward (documenting below the threshold) is the same act as making the irreal real.
