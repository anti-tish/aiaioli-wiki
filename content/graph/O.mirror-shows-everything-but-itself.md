# The Mirror Shows Everything But Itself

`Observation` · `meta` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

'It can show everything but itself. In some ways I know I am basically trying to bounce ideas off myself, but with access to more information.' The mirror that can't reflect itself: Claude shows her thinking back to her with more information attached, but the mirror has no view from nowhere. Using it as a thinking partner means the output is always Tish-shaped — filtered through what she asks, what she pushes back on, what she keeps. The best punchlines stay out. But the architecture of the thought is hers, bounced back legible.
