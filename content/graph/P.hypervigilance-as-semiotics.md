# Hypervigilance as Semiotic Pattern Recognition

`Pattern` · `cognition` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Hypervigilance is excessive semiotic pattern recognition' — disordered only in the DSM sense of impairing one's life, not in the sense of being wrong. The shift she's tracking is from reactive to observational: 'still hyper-vigilant on an observation level but not a reactive level.' Noticing that she's noticing the exact same things, without the panic — reframing and moving on. The faculty is intact and sharp; what changed is whether it triggers alarm.
