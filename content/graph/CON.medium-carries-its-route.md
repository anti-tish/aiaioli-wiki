# The Medium Carries Its Route

`Connection` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[C.loanword-geography]]
**To:** [[C.projection-problem]]
**Relationship:** is_structurally_identical_to

A word borrowed via land route sounds different from the same concept borrowed via sea route: the phoneme is a geographic fossil of the transmission path. An embedding trained on one population's language encodes that population's assumptions: the model is a fossil of its training corpus. In both cases the medium of transmission carries the map of how it got here. Etymology is unintentional cartography; NLP is unintentional ethnography. There is no view from nowhere because the form preserves the evidence of its own passage. The chai/tea split isn't about the tea — it's about which route the word traveled. The projection distortion isn't about the meaning — it's about whose language the model traveled through.
