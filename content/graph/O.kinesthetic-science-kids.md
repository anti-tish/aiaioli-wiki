# Kinesthetic Knowing Before Vocabulary

`Observation` · `cognition` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Kids who are good at science aren't necessarily book smarter — I think they have a stronger kinesthetic understanding. Like knowing something is denser because it moves faster when you throw it, before you know what density or speed even mean.' A claim that physical intuition precedes and underwrites formal knowledge, possibly universal in children with some keeping the channel open. Of a piece with her love of the 'physical, structural, chemical properties of shit.'
