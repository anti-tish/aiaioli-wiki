# Armor / Play / Signal

`Architecture` · `aesthetics` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

The wardrobe framework, built explicitly around how her autism manifests: clothing serves three jobs at once — armor (feel untouchable), play (creative expression), signal (communicate something specific). Silhouette is a mix of hard and soft; palette is a dark base plus one wild card (black, navy, espresso, hunter green — the exact colors of the four high-end bags she owns); acquisition is thrift/vintage-hunter, because buying new in-store is 'joyless compared to the thrill of the hunt.' A system for getting dressed that routes around decision-paralysis the same way the knowledge graph routes around the blank page.
