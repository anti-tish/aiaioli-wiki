# Decorum Is Gendered Enforcement

`Connection` · `comedy_performance` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[P.women-dont-get-to-be-gross]]
**To:** [[C.decorum-as-policing]]
**Relationship:** is_instance_of

C.decorum-as-policing: 'the current rules of propriety are policing in nature' — decorum is a mechanism for controlling who gets to speak how, dressed as manners. P.women-dont-get-to-be-gross: women don't get to be a little gross the way men do; when they are, it becomes evidence of being a bad woman, not just a gross comedian. The pattern is the general principle; the observation is the gendered application — decorum's enforcement is not uniform, it is targeted. The Aristocrats is canon because it's DUDE: not because it's a different KIND of gross, but because the same grossness didn't get policed out of them. The enforcement asymmetry IS the policing mechanism revealing its actual function. The standard isn't about decorum; it's about who is permitted outside the standard.
