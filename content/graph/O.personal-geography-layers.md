# Personal Geography Layers

`Observation` · `geography` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

Geography has at least four distinct layers that can be modeled separately and queried together: physical (coordinates, distance), political (containment — Tampa in Florida in USA), cultural (trade routes, linguistic spread, shared history), personal (places I care about, interest level, memory anchors). Traditional GIS conflates these or treats them as attribute columns. A knowledge graph can model them as distinct relationship types that can be traversed independently or jointly. This is the mechanism by which 'geographic thinking as cognitive architecture' becomes technically implementable.
