# Russian Russian vs Americanized Russian (the Krusty Register)

`Observation` · `comedy` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **working**

Her phone keyboard got stuck in Russian. The bit has two layers. The base is a self-own riding a stupid stereotype — the tech-illiterate person who accidentally bricks their device into a foreign language: *"oh I'm so bad I got it stuck in Russian!"* The elevation is the tell that she's anything but: she didn't even know if it was *Russian Russian or Americanized Russian* — the whole question of romanizing Cyrillic, transliteration, post-Soviet/SSR-state variants. Her own read: "a PG version of slapstick before elevated. Like that but on krusty the clown."

A distinct register of [[O.slapstick-before-elevated]]: the elevation isn't reference-literacy (no Ligotti, no LaTeX), it's playing dumb out loud while a hyper-specific knowledge leaks through the seams — delivered broad and vaudeville rather than sly. The self-deprecation is the slapstick; the romanization deep-cut is the elevation. ("Is that funny to anyone but me?" — the niche is the point.)
