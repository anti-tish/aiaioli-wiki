# Proto-, Not Post-

`Pattern` · `language` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Post-colonial' makes the timeline feel finished — the colonizers acted, and now we live in the aftermath. 'Proto-apocalyptic' implicates current actors: the apocalypse hasn't happened yet, which means the people building toward it are still building toward it now. 'Post-' forecloses accountability; 'proto-' reinstates it. Applied directly: climate perpetrators are proto-apocalyptic actors, not characters in a finished postcolonial narrative. The prefix IS the political move. Also applies to postcolonial literature's slide into speculative fiction — it was already pre- (proto-) all along.
