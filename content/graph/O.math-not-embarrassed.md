# Better at Math Now

`Observation` · `learning` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

On finally turning toward math after a life of math anxiety: 'I feel like I am better at math now than when I was younger, if only because I'm not embarrassed about not understanding something anymore.' The anxiety was social, not innate — 'tough parents, peak of STEM is the only real career,' math as a hurdle rather than a place. The reframe is that the interest was always there and the environment pushed it out; what changed isn't ability but the willingness to sit in not-understanding, which is the actual prerequisite.
