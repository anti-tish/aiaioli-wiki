# Missionary Maps as Reconnaissance

`Observation` · `geography` · *confident* · visibility: **publishable**

Portuguese Jesuits mapping Ethiopia from the 1500s onward. The maps were not neutral scientific documents — they were reconnaissance. They noted resource locations (gold, ivory), population centers (conversion targets), political structures (who to ally with, who to undermine), and routes (invasion/trade planning). The same Jesuit was often both cartographer and diplomat. The map served evangelization AND colonial interests simultaneously. The two weren't in tension — they were the same operation. This is the empirical foundation for P.geographic-knowledge-neutral: not a critique, a description of how cartography has always worked.
