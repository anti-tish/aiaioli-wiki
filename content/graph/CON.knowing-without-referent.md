# Knowing Without a Referent

`Connection` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[P.reason-before-name]]
**To:** [[O.projection-problem-realization]]
**Relationship:** is_unresolved_tension_with

P.reason-before-name says you can know something fully before you have language for it — the structure arrives before the vocabulary; she refuses to wait for her language's permission. O.projection-problem-realization says there is no external referent for semantic space: you cannot detect your own distortions because 'the model's version of meaning IS the space.' Tension: P.reason-before-name is epistemically confident in pre-verbal knowing (the knot is real; the reason is there even before you can name it). O.projection-problem-realization is epistemically pessimistic about unverified knowing (hallucination is unsolvable the same way map distortion is: you pick what you're willing to lie about). Can you trust the pre-verbal reason if you have no way to detect when it's distorted? One says: the pre-linguistic structure is trustworthy. The other says: there is no outside position from which to check. This tension is not resolvable within the graph — it's a real epistemic problem.
