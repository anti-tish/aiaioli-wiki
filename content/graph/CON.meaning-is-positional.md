# Meaning Is Positional

`Connection` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[C.latent-space]]
**To:** [[C.relational-ontology]]
**Relationship:** is_structurally_identical_to

C.latent-space: meaning emerges from position in a high-dimensional geometric space; a word is defined by its neighbors, not by any intrinsic property — Tobler's First Law in 768 dimensions. C.relational-ontology: identity and meaning emerge from relationships, not substance; relation is the precondition for there being a world at all. Both: meaning is not in the thing but in the relations between things. The model's vector mathematics and Tish's philosophical stake are saying the same thing about where meaning lives. One is the formal machinery; one is the founding claim. The coincidence isn't metaphorical — they converged on the same answer because the answer is correct. A one-entity universe has nothing to measure meaning against; a vector with no neighbors has no meaning either.
