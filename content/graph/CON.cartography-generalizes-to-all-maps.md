# Cartography Generalizes to All Maps

`Connection` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[P.same-structure-domains]]
**To:** [[C.critical-cartography]]
**Relationship:** generalizes

C.critical-cartography is Harley's argument about maps specifically: the mapmaker is never absent, projection is never neutral, power hides inside what looks like objective geometry. P.same-structure-domains is the claim that this analysis applies not just to maps but to every knowledge structure — embedding spaces, taxonomies, algorithms, arguments, comedy sets. Critical cartography IS the thesis, localized to one domain. P.same-structure-domains is what happens when you ask: what if Harley is right not just about maps but about all systems that claim to represent the world? The generalization isn't a stretch — it's Harley's own logic applied at the next zoom level. Every representation encodes the position of whoever made it. The map is just the place where this became undeniable first. This CON node uses `generalizes` — the directional inverse of `is_instance_of` — to name the upward move explicitly, because the move is contestable: you can argue that P.same-structure-domains oversteps Harley's argument, that cartographic epistemology doesn't transfer to comedy or cognition. The edge is a claim, not a fact.
