# Ligature Cognition

`Observation` · `cognition` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Ligatures are really the key. I know all of my individual letters are influenced by the whole word.' Handwriting is contextual, not character-by-character: each letter is shaped by the letters around it. Standard OCR and font systems treat characters as independent units; the actual cognitive act is holistic. The word is the unit, not the letter. An observation with implications for how we build input systems, recognition systems, and why character-level processing systematically misses what makes handwriting human.
