# Trade Routes Visible in Language

`Pattern` · `geography` · *confident* · visibility: **publishable**

Trade routes leave traces in language as readable as physical infrastructure. The route determines which dialect a word passes through, and the phoneme encodes that contact. The word is cargo. Instances: Russian чай (chai) came overland via the Silk Road from Mandarin 茶 (chá); English 'tea' came by sea through Dutch traders from Min Nan 'te.' Same concept, different pronunciation, because the route determined which Chinese dialect it passed through. 'Bazaar' came to Russian via Persian along the same Silk Road as chai — two words from different source languages, same route, same mechanism. Etymology is a form of cartography. 'Phoneme as fossil' — you can read the trade route in the phoneme.
