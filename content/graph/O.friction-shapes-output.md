# Friction Shapes Output

`Observation` · `writing` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

'When I finally removed the friction, the output was immense.' She wrote 352 pages of journal in 11 months via Diarly without 'trying to write.' The form — stream-of-consciousness, geo-tagged, timestamped, mobile, no minimum length, no audience — removed friction entirely. The output was immense. She had believed she couldn't write prolifically; the form revealed she was 'selectively blocked.' The ream-of-paper version of the same writing would have produced paralysis. Key realization: the container shapes the output. Blank Word doc → paralysis. App that captures the thought and moves on → manuscript. Applied insight: the same mechanism predicts success or failure for any creative practice.
