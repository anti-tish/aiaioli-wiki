# Infantilizing, Not Childish

`Concept` · `rhetoric` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

A precise distinction about why certain framings make her prickle: the cringe 'didn't feel childish. It felt infantilizing. Big difference.' She wouldn't explain a hard thing to a kid with 'small destructions' either — the problem isn't simplicity, it's condescension. Crystallized as a rule: 'thesis 1: don't dumb it down.' The faculty that recoils at being talked down to is the same one that powers the comedy: the audience gets treated as adults who can keep up.
