# Spatial-Is-Special Is the Problem

`Pattern` · `geography_cartography` · *hypothesis* · register: **public** · auto-extracted, not yet author-verified

"Spatial is special" is geography's favorite self-defense, and it functions as the mechanism of
public spatial illiteracy. A field that treats its knowledge as requiring special tools, special
data models, and special training produces a public that can't use spatial reasoning at all —
most Americans don't know their county runs a GIS department, and where one exists it's filed
under the property assessor. The exceptionalism built a priesthood; the priesthood built the
ignorance. Spatial is special in exactly one register — the pinned one
(`C.pinned-vs-represented` *(held)*) — and extending the claim everywhere else is how the public was
taught that maps are finished.
