# We Think in Helvetica

`Concept` · `cognition` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'We think in Helvetica.' The default typeface isn't neutral — it's a cognitive interface imposed by whoever decided what the screen default was. The observation arrived from asking why a personal-handwriting dynamic font doesn't exist: not because it's technically hard, but because the assumption is that text = Helvetica, period. Connects to her critique of Markdown (semantic mismatch) and her preference for tools that fit her actual cognitive shape rather than asking her to map onto a standardized one.
