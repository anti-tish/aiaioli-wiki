# Proxy Fails at Physical Constraint

`Pattern` · `political_economy` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Currency is no longer an effective proxy for material wealth or power in the exclusively resource-limited economy, and debt as a control mechanism is a universal failure.' Financial and political instruments (currency, debt, national budgets) are proxies for underlying physical resources. When those resources hit hard limits — Iran shutting the Strait of Hormuz, climate-driven scarcity — the proxies lose their signaling function. Redistributing financial claims doesn't solve the upstream constraint. The lever doesn't reach the thing it was supposed to move.
