# Medium Is Antagonist

`Pattern` · `writing` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

Across her formal experiments, the medium/form fights the narrator or content rather than serving it. The constraint isn't incidental — it's the point. Instances: the GitHub horror story (version control becomes infected; the code IS the threat), the Tufte organ story (margin notes escalate until they consume the main text; form as visual power struggle), forced first/third person switching (narrator loses control of perspective; the POV shift IS the crisis), slideshow comedy (presenter fights the presentation; medium rebels in real time), the journal's pronoun-switching (grammar as self-regulation tool; the medium creates the distance). The pattern captures her recurring obsession: not an unreliable narrator (lying/confused) but a narrator who cannot fully control the medium they're working in. This also names her existential dilemma — the medium is always antagonist to the inner experience it tries to capture.
