# The Gender War Thesis

`Concept` · `knowledge_systems` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'All war is not class war, all war is the gender war.' Framed not as a social claim but an information-completeness argument: centuries of systematic exclusion of women (and non-conforming men) from recorded deaths, incidents, and decision-making during conflict means the historical record is a biased sample. Downstream conclusions built on that sample — including class-war theory — propagate the incompleteness. The thesis is less about gender as cause and more about completeness failure. The data never captured the full system; the analysis is therefore structurally partial.
