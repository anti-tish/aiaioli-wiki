# Projection Problem Realization

`Observation` · `AI` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

In geography, real-world parameters flag when a calculation is wildly wrong — space is finite, topology enforced, unit-bound. In semantic space there is no equivalent external referent. The model's version of meaning IS the space. Errors propagate undetectably. You cannot tell you're in a bad projection without stepping outside it and there is no outside. This is why hallucination is unsolvable in the same way map distortion is unsolvable: you have to pick what you're willing to lie about, and in semantic space you don't even know what you started with.
