# Church Observation Economy

`Concept` · `history` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

Her theory: the Catholic church was the first systematized observation economy — 'getting there first and claiming it in the name of God while also systematizing extraction of any and all resources.' The church didn't just endorse extraction; it built the original framework of legitimate institutional surveillance and resource concentration under divine sanction. The secular observation economy — Silicon Valley's data collection, late-stage capitalism's bookkeeper-gatekeeper stack — inherited the architecture, not just the impulse. Correction she makes: the quant/accounting formalization of this came more from Protestants ('the quant origin story on the protestants. i know that to be true irl, but never let the facts get in the way of a good story 🥲'). Philip Jenkins's work on 'rhetorical concentration' — institutions concentrating meaning-making in themselves — is the academic frame she needs for this argument.
