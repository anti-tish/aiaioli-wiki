# Naming the Position

`Connection` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[C.shame-disinfectant]]
**To:** [[C.critical-cartography]]
**Relationship:** is_structurally_identical_to

Same operative move, different domain. Critical cartography (Harley): the mapmaker is never absent from the map; the honest cartographer names their position rather than pretending to neutral geometry. Shame-disinfectant: the speaker is never shameless; the honest speaker names their mess rather than pretending to clean authority. Both: radical transparency about the position you speak from destroys the fiction of the neutral account. The move is identical — claim your location, collapse the pretense of nowhere. Critical cartography applies this to knowledge production; shame-disinfectant applies it to social performance. Neither is metaphor for the other. They are the same operation in different registers, running on the same logic: the power of the hostile thing (the hostile projection, the shame weapon) depends on the pretense of its necessity. Name it, and the power breaks.
