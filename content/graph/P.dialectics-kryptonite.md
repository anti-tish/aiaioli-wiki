# Dialectics as Social Kryptonite

`Pattern` · `rhetoric` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

A move she's honed living among contrarians: the way to beat someone who reflexively argues is to leave them nothing to argue against. 'If you can prevent them from even having anything to say devil's advocate against, they can't put the onus on you.' Pinpoint precision is the weapon — say it so exactly that there is no technicality to seize and no weak comparison to attack. She calls it 'social kryptonite,' and ties it loosely to her standing interest in rhetoric and dialectics: win by removing the surface, not by out-shouting.
