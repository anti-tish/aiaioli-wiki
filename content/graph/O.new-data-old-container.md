# New Data, Old Container

`Observation` · `geography_cartography` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **working**

Verification (2026-06-17): tools that put Wikidata on a map already exist, several times over — the
WDQS built-in Map view (`#defaultView:Map`), the academic *Wikidata Atlas* (del Pino & Hogan), the
*Wikidata Geo-Explorer*, and the Wikimedia-Deutschland *Wikidata Map*. So the 'rudimentary atlas of
semantic data' is built; do not rebuild it. But every one of them is the *old container with new data
poured in*: the richest relational dataset on Earth, displayed as dots on a basemap — datum intact,
zoom = scale, one dot per place, no superposition, no chosen geometry. They take the new substrate and
pin it straight back onto S². They do not refute the thesis; they *are* it — exhibit A, not
competition. The contribution was never 'put Wikidata on a map' (done); it is 'stop treating the map as
the container.'
