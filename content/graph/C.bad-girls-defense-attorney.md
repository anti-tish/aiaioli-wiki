# Bad Girls Defense Attorney

`Concept` · `writing` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

Her frame for her structural feminist comedy practice: defending women who refuse to perform acceptability. Not 'empowerment' feminism (lean in, girl boss) but the more confrontational position: 'What if we just stopped apologizing for existing in ways that make people uncomfortable?' The mechanism: 'bad girls' are those who are loud, messy, unashamed, non-apologetic — and the designation of 'bad' is what disqualifies them from defense and sympathy, even when they are victims. The bad girls defense attorney makes shame (not accountability) the punchline by refusing the disqualification. If you won't look at the system making the designation, you're defending purity politics — and that's funny in a different way. The frame extends across racial and class lines: the same enforcement logic keeps people 'at their station' regardless of which category is policed.
