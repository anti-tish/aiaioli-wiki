# A Tight Atom Buys a Wide System

`Pattern` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Set the tiniest possible parameters so you can have the greatest range of acceptable outcomes.' First: a unit is meaningless without its frame — you have to know what '1x1' means in the projection you're working in before any further calculation has a referent, because 1x1 is a different real-world size in every projection. Then the counterintuitive move: pinning the smallest unit *tightly* does not narrow your outcomes, it maximizes them. A few rigid definitions at the base license an unbounded space of valid wholes above — the way a small set of axioms generates infinite theorems. Constrain the atom and you free the system; leave it vague and nothing above it is trustworthy. This is the operational engine underneath going-to-the-grid-first: precision at the bottom is what buys range at the top, so the move that *looks* like reductive fussing is actually the one that opens the most room.

**Restated for knowledge itself (live session 2026-06-26), and she stands by it:** 'depth is the only place the unbenchmarkable lives, and structure is the machine that turns depth into breadth.' Mapping out depth builds networks with breadth — you don't survey your way to range, you earn it from the tight atom and let the relations spread it. (Banked as a claim about *structure*; the personal-method version — that this was ever her intentional aim — she left unbanked on purpose.)
