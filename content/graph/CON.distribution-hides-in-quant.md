# Distribution Hides in Quant

`Connection` · `political_economy` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[C.distribution-of-suffering]]
**To:** [[C.quant-observation-economy]]
**Relationship:** is_structurally_identical_to

C.distribution-of-suffering: tradition-as-justification is a distribution map dressed as natural law; suffering gets routed toward whoever can least refuse it, and the routing looks like inevitability. C.quant-observation-economy: 'all quantitative work serves the purpose of flattening and extraction'; famines are constructed; cost and currency make artificial scarcity feel like natural law. Both: a distribution choice — who bears what cost — is hidden inside an abstraction (tradition, numbers, natural law). The quant economy doesn't say 'we decided to route this cost here'; it says 'this is the price.' The suffering is real. The distribution is a decision. The abstraction makes the decision invisible. The mechanism is the same in both cases; the quant economy is the contemporary, large-scale implementation of the same structure C.distribution-of-suffering describes in labor terms.
