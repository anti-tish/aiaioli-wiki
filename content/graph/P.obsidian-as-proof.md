# Obsidian as Proof

`Pattern` · `knowledge_systems` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'Obsidian exists privately and is still user-centric, free, and making huge improvements — shows it can be done, period.' Obsidian (and VLC) as the counterexample to the claim that user-centric software requires VC scale. The business model is durably private; the product is getting better, not worse. The existence proof matters: it demolishes "it can't be done" as an argument. The community architecture is the key variable — not ownership structure alone, but whether users are treated as participants or product.
