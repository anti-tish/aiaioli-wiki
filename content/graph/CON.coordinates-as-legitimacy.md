# Coordinates as Legitimacy

`Connection` · `institutions` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[O.church-determines-mappable]]
**To:** [[P.institutional-knowledge-legitimacy]]
**Relationship:** is_instance_of

The Catholic Church's control of cartography is the historical founding instance of P.institutional-knowledge-legitimacy. The church determined what was mappable — which territories deserved coordinates, which peoples existed in the geographic record — and that determination function IS how institutional knowledge control converts distribution decisions into natural law. To be unmapped was to be unrecognized. The coordinate was not neutral geometry; it was consecration. The pattern (institution controls what counts as credible knowledge; that control disappears inside the mechanism of knowledge itself; the gatekeeper role makes itself necessary then invisible) is three centuries older than Silicon Valley and has the papal seal to show for it.
