# Modes Converged

`Observation` · `writing` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

The aiaioli methodology paragraph started as a note-to-self to process Claude's response. It became outside voice when the context the self needed to tell itself the story turned out to be exactly the context the imagined reader would need too. The audiences aligned. Translation cost dropped to near zero — not through effort, but because both endpoints needed the same information. Tish didn't switch modes; the modes converged. The move isn't 'write for a reader' — it's 'find the prompt that makes you need to tell yourself the story from the beginning.' When that condition is met, the audience distinction collapses. Internal and external voice are the same document.
