# Associative, Not Linear Structure

`Observation` · `writing` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

Her natural comedic and narrative structure is: associative (this reminds me of this, which connects to this), recursive (circling back with new context), and unresolved (the contradiction IS the point). This is not 'bad storytelling' — it's a different structure. She thinks in constellations, not lines. Bits connect through emotional resonance and thematic echoes, not chronology or narrative logic. 'The funny happens DURING the journey, not at the end.' Comparisons: Tig Notaro's deadpan meandering, Maria Bamford's associative leaps, Mike Birbiglia's 'one more thing' spiral, Mitch Hedberg's non-sequitur accumulation (which has an emotional arc even if not narrative). The tonal arc replaces the story arc. She identified this as a source of confusion (thinking it was a weakness) until it was reframed as a voice.
