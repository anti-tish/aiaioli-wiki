# Invitation with Consequences

`Concept` · `writing` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

Her core comedy method. Structure: present an uncomfortable truth → hold the discomfort without softening or apologizing → observe the audience's response → if they engage, you're examining together; if they refuse, the refusal becomes the illustration of why the examination is needed. 'Either way, I win.' The invitation is genuine — the examination includes Tish herself, and the consequence is mild (discomfort, not humiliation). What's targeted is the CHOICE to defend a system rather than look at it. Without stakes, observational humor is just nice. With stakes, it's art that demands something. Adjacent to Ziwe (silence after uncomfortable question; your squirming is my material), Gadsby's Nanette, Carlin. Distinct from mean comedy: not lying, includes herself in the critique, targets choices not immutable traits. 'Pedagogical moment disguised as comedy.'
