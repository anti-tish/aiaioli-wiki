# Title Captures the Mechanism

`Connection` · `tools` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

**From:** [[O.capture-friction-title]]
**To:** [[P.capture-tools-fail]]
**Relationship:** is_instance_of

P.capture-tools-fail says capture tools fail by requiring structure before thought. O.capture-friction-title says the mandatory title field in Apple Notes was the specific friction point that broke capture. The title requirement IS the pattern's prediction: the tool asked for categorization (naming) at the moment of capture, before the thought was understood. This is why you can't title a thought that just arrived — at capture, the thought doesn't yet know what it is. That's the whole point of capturing it. The observation diagnosed the mechanism from a specific bad UX decision; the pattern generalizes it. This is also the origin of the whole system architecture — Drafts → Diarly → Claude extraction — emerging from a single Apple Notes annoyance. The smallest friction broke the loop.
