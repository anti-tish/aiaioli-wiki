# Geography as Bastard Child

`Concept` · `geography` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

'I've never really understood the weird positioning of geography in the social and physical sciences. It's such a bastard child and no one claims us lol.' The field sits in an institutional no-man's-land — too quantitative for humanities, too qualitative for hard sciences, too spatial for either. The people who were actually good at it went to tech and stopped calling themselves geographers. What remained was an institutional shell. The ideas, meanwhile, are load-bearing in every field that deals with space, distribution, or observation — they're just scattered and unattributed.
