# The 24-Hour Bodega as Community Cornerstone

`Concept` · `urban_planning` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

An urban-planning theory she's been fomenting: the actual cornerstone of a community should be the 24-hour bodega / corner store / neighborhood market — basic first aid and pantry staples, always open — with formalized community space immediately next in the planning transect. The place for provisions deserves to be more formalized than current zoning treats it. A geographer's claim about what infrastructure a neighborhood is actually built around.
