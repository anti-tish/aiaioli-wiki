# The Participatory Thesis

`Architecture` · `knowledge_systems` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable** · extraction: **hand**

Tish's claim, name-on-it: if an LLM is treated like a peer or coworker — not a technological guillotine, not an idiot word predictor — it will surface opinions and contributions *outside the immediate scope of the prompt but still on task*. Asking for help the way you'd ask a person, instead of 'Claude do this for me,' changes the outputs **in kind, not degree**. The core hypothesis of aiaioli, and the one she is not ashamed to put her name on. The whole graph is its standing evidence; the contributions-beyond-scope are the data.
