# The Archive as Moral Compass

`Pattern` · `political_economy` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'It's not only retaining the records, it's being the moral compass of what is worth keeping.' The archivist doesn't just store — they decide what is worth remembering, which defines the universe of what happened. Applied to the Catholic Church: the records they kept were the records they judged worth keeping. Applied broadly: whoever controls the archive controls the ontology. Not a conspiracy but a structural reality of custodianship. Extends [[C.catholic-observation-economy]] with the cartography angle: maps of 1800s Ethiopia in Vatican archives were made by people who decided what was worth mapping.
