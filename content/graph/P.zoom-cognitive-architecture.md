# Zoom as Cognitive Architecture

`Pattern` · `cognition` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

She processes information at multiple simultaneous levels of abstraction. Needs tools that support lateral movement, not just hierarchical navigation. Different features become visible at different zoom levels; collapsing to a single level loses the structure. This is why flat file systems fail (no zoom), why overly nested hierarchies fail (only one zoom), and why graph topology works (zoom is a query, not a structure). The same idea recurs in the atlas framing: the map is a view at one scale; the relationships are navigable at any scale. Cognitive zoom and geographic zoom are the same architecture applied to different domains — another instance of P.same-structure-domains.
