# Humor as Armor and Weapon

`Observation` · `writing` · *personal_truth* · visibility: **publishable**

Humor functions simultaneously as armor and weapon — and these aren't in tension, they're the same mechanism. As armor: when someone doesn't laugh, it isn't just 'the joke didn't land' — it's 'my protection didn't work.' As weapon: it's how systems get examined, edges kept sharp, discomfort created. The dual function explains the entitlement-to-laughter block: if humor is primarily armor, then audience non-response is experienced as a threat, not data. It also explains why she bristles at 'you're here for my entertainment' — humor-as-weapon is about Tish having power; humor-as-commodity inverts that. Resolution: distinguishing comedy-as-art (public offering, works some of the time for some people) from humor-as-defense (personal, needs to work reliably for safety). Comedy as art doesn't threaten the armor because it's not the armor — it's a separate gift.
