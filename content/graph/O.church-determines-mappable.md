# Church Determines Mappable

`Observation` · `geography` · *confident* · visibility: **publishable**

The Church didn't just keep maps — they determined what was *mappable*. What deserved coordinates. What was 'wilderness' vs. 'settlement.' What place names to use (replacing indigenous names with saint names). By controlling the epistemological framework for what counted as geographic knowledge, they shaped what was considered real space vs. terra incognita. Ethiopian political boundaries, resource locations, ethnic territories — all filtered through the interpretive lens of whoever held the surveying equipment and archive access. This is C.lawful-evil operating in the cartographic domain: the institution doesn't just use knowledge for power — it determines what counts as knowledge.
