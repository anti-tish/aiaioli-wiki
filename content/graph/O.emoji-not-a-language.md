# Emoji Rides Unicode But Isn't a Language

`Observation` · `communication` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **working**

Emoji being unicode is actually a big evolution in communication — in her read, **the first standardized tone indicators in text.** There is now a way to denote *exactly* which level of smiley you are, and it works across every platform. A universal *encoding* (unicode) produced a universal *tone layer*, carried everywhere the standard reaches. But even though most/all devices can do unicode, emoji is usually not considered a language — and it isn't a formal one at this time. The infrastructure crossed the boundary; the taxonomy didn't follow.
