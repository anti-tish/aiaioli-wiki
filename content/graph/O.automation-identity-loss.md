# Automation as Identity Loss

`Observation` · `political_economy` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

'If you gave people infinite time and money, they would almost universally do exactly what they do today. I'm not sure I wouldn't be in there. I do think on some level that's the fear. A loss of job as identity.' The automation anxiety isn't really about survival — it's about what work does for identity. Infinite leisure doesn't solve it because the problem isn't scarcity; it's the absence of a legible social role. The fear is structural, not economic.
