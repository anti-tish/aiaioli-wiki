# Same Structure, Different Domains

`Pattern` · `epistemology` · *hypothesis* · visibility: **publishable**

The same structure appears across domains at different zoom levels. This is arguably the thesis: Zodiac = butterfly effect. Church = algorithm. Map = semiotic system. Words = raster stacks. Embedding space = geographic projection. The move — finding structural identity across apparently unrelated domains — is the core cognitive operation repeated throughout Tish's thinking. Not analogy (one domain illuminates another) but structural equivalence (same mechanism, different substrate). The geographic frame isn't a metaphor for knowledge systems — they ARE the same system running at different scales. Named here because the recurrence is the point: once you see it, you can't unsee it. Every cluster of observations in this graph is a candidate for P.same-structure-domains.
