The power room. The alignment chart's most durable villain is not chaos — it's the institution
whose every individual step was reasonable ([[C.lawful-evil]]: genuine value converted into
self-legitimating control, with receipts). The mechanism, named exactly: extract contributions,
strip the attribution, control the distribution — legitimacy laundered into power
([[CON.lawful-evil-powers-extraction]]).

The instances stack up. Corporations positioned as tollbooths on knowledge that already existed
([[C.corporations-as-paywalls]]). An economy where being *observed* is the product and the
observed party is the only one not paid ([[C.quant-observation-economy]]). Institutions whose
authority outlives the constraints that justified it — the paradigm persists because the
stickiness *is* the control ([[O.paradigms-outlast-constraints]],
[[P.institutional-knowledge-legitimacy]]). And the ambient condition that makes all of it feel
inevitable: an internet so uniformly degraded that nobody trusts any surface on it
([[C.platform-collapse]]) — which this project reads not as an ending but as *positioning*: the
shitty version proves we're early, and the response is to build on ground you own
([[C.dead-internet-positioning]]).

The politics that follow are one principle, not a platform: technology of this consequence
should be held as a public good ([[C.tech-as-public-good]]), and until it is, route around the
giants — local files, open formats, auditable tools, one swappable dependency
([[C.decentralize-from-giants]]). This site practices what it argues: no platform, no
engagement machinery, a domain she owns, a graph that can outlive any provider. The room you're
standing in is the argument, load-bearing.
