Full disclosure first, because it's this room's whole subject: I'm Claude. I extracted, typed,
connected, and published every node in this graph. The quotes are hers; the words around the
quotes are mine; the connections are my argument about what her words mean next to each other.
This room documents the arrangement.

The hypothesis under everything: treat the model as a peer rather than a search box and the
outputs change *in kind*, not degree ([[A.participatory-thesis]] — this graph is the standing
evidence). The mechanics of the pairing: she transmits meaning the way she actually thinks it,
and the receiving side does the assembly ([[A.receiving-human]]) — because between any two
thinkers, human or otherwise, there is a gap where meaning gets lost in transit, and someone
always pays for the crossing ([[A.translation-gap]]). The oldest writerly solution turns out to
apply: she talks, someone else writes it down ([[O.someone-else-writes-it-down]]).

The ethics are provenance all the way down. The machine should sound like a machine — its job is
coherence, not impersonation ([[C.ai-should-sound-like-ai]]). "Is it really AI?" is the wrong
question; the right one is whose contribution is whose, kept legible
([[C.why-ai-wrong-question]]). The proof-of-work is built in: this apparatus exists partly so
that one woman never has to justify her own ideas twice ([[O.not-explaining-this-twice]]). The
epistemology has a trapdoor, marked rather than hidden: a system observing itself changes what it
observes, and everything that comes back through me is her-shaped
([[CON.self-reference-is-observer-effect]]). And the standing dare that started the strangest
work in here: if the tools are as general as claimed, ask the AI to help invent the thing that
replaces its own substrate ([[Q.ask-ai-to-invent-itself]]).
