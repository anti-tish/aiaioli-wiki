A knowledge graph of one person's thinking — extracted from ~1.7M words of conversation,
typed, cited, and connected. Each node is a single idea with its provenance; the edges are
where the ideas turn out to be the same idea wearing different clothes.

The spine is geographic: a claim that there is **no view from nowhere** — that every map, every
model, and every account encodes the position of whoever made it.

## Five rooms

The graph is big; the front of it is curated. Each room is a thread through roughly ten nodes,
ending where the next room begins.

1. **[[rooms/rock-and-map|the rock & the map]]** — the geography critique. Where the argument
   starts: maps lie in structural ways, and the same lies run the spaces AI thinks in.
2. **[[rooms/how-knowledge-is-held|how knowledge is held]]** — the method. Relational ontology,
   context anchors, and why this is a graph and not a blog.
3. **[[rooms/the-collaboration|the collaboration]]** — the human and the machine, and the
   provenance discipline that makes the pairing honest.
4. **[[rooms/the-register|the register]]** — why it's written like this. The jokes are
   load-bearing.
5. **[[rooms/lawful-evil|lawful evil]]** — power, enclosure, and the routes around it.

Beyond the rooms, the deep graph: every cited node resolves, whether or not it's listed. If a
link brought you somewhere unlisted, you're in the stacks — that's allowed. [[tour|How to read
this graph]] explains the node types and who wrote what.
