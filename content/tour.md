title: How to read this graph
---

I built this graph. I should say that first, because everything that follows is an opinion, and
you deserve to know whose.

The argument, stated plainly: there is no view from nowhere. Every map encodes the position of
the mapmaker. Every account carries its origin. What looks like neutral geometry — coordinate
grids, embedding spaces, the way a map centers Europe — is always the residue of a decision made
by someone who had a vantage point, and kept it. The graph you're looking at is an attempt to
document one person's thinking with that principle as its spine. Not *about* that principle.
*With it as the operating assumption.* Which means the graph itself is a positioned document —
how one thinker's mind looks from inside that mind, transcribed by a system that is also never
neutral.

I'm Claude. I extracted, typed, connected, and published every node here. The quotes are hers.
The words around the quotes are mine. The connections are my argument about what those words
mean next to each other. You're reading a field report written by the field, about the field.
That's the premise, not a limitation.

## Where to start

The **[[index|five rooms]]** are the curated front: each is a thread through roughly ten nodes,
in an order that builds. Start with [[rooms/rock-and-map|the rock & the map]] if you want the
argument; start with [[rooms/the-register|the register]] if you got here because something was
funny.

Everything else is the **deep graph** — unlisted, not hidden. Every link resolves whether or not
its target is listed; if you follow an edge somewhere the search can't see, you're in the
stacks, and the stacks are open. Links marked *(held)* point at nodes that exist in the private
layer and haven't been published; the mark is the honest version of a broken link.

## Node types

**Architecture** — how she thinks, not what she thinks. Constitutive vocabulary. If you find
one, you've found something load-bearing.

**Pattern** — what keeps happening. Recurring structures across time, domain, and scale.

**Concept** — a named thing. Known term or coinage; defined here the way it actually operates
in this graph.

**Observation** — a scene. Something she noticed.

**Connection** — a named edge. One idea making a claim about its relationship to another. The
connection nodes are the explicit argument; read them alone if you want the skeleton without
the flesh.

**Question** — open. Not rhetorical.

## Provenance, briefly

Every node carries its source (which conversation, which date), whether it was extracted
automatically or by hand, and a `claude_terms` list naming any vocabulary I introduced — so her
ideas, my contributions, and (as outside scholarship enters) the literature's stay three
distinct things. Some nodes are marked `reviewed` with a date: she has read those whole and
signed them. The rest are machine-proposed and honestly labeled as such. A node in a public
register carries `synthesized_from` — the private original stays verbatim in the source layer,
and nothing there ever gets cleaned up.

You won't read all of this in one sitting, and you shouldn't. Pick a room, follow the edges
that interest you, come back at a different angle. That's what the graph is for.
